Ice Cream

Ice cream is a frozen dessert made from a mixture of milk, cream, sugar, and other flavorings. Its origins can be traced back to ancient China and Persia, where people would mix snow and ice with fruit and other flavorings to create a refreshing treat. The modern version of ice cream is believed to have been first created in Italy during the Renaissance, when recipes for iced desserts made from milk, cream, and sugar began to appear in cookbooks. Ice cream became increasingly popular throughout Europe and North America during the 18th and 19th centuries, and it has since become a beloved dessert all around the world. Today, there are countless varieties of ice cream, with a wide range of flavors, toppings, and mix-ins.

prep time

15 min.

cook time

10 min.

serving

4-6 servings

Tags : Breakfast Lunch Dinner Dessert

instructions

step 1

In a medium saucepan, heat the heavy cream, whole milk, and sugar over medium heat until the sugar has dissolved, stirring occasionally.

step 2

Remove from heat and stir in the vanilla extract and salt.

step 3

Let the mixture cool to room temperature.

step 4

Pour the mixture into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer's instructions.

step 5

Transfer the ice cream to a freezer-safe container and freeze for several hours, or until firm.

ingredients

2 cups heavy cream

1 cup whole milk

3/4 cup granulated sugar

1 tablespoon vanilla extract

Pinch of salt

tools

Medium saucepan

Whisk

Measuring cups and spoons

Ice cream maker

Freezer-safe container
