Omelette

Omelettes originated in ancient Persia, spread to the Middle East and Europe, and evolved into a popular dish in French cuisine. They are made by beating eggs and cooking them with various fillings. Omelettes are a great source of protein, customizable, and enjoyed as a breakfast dish or light meal/snack.

prep time

10 min.

cook time

10 min.

serving

1-2 servings

Tags : Breakfast Lunch Dinner Dessert

instructions

step 1

Crack two eggs into a small mixing bowl and season with salt and pepper. Beat the eggs with a fork or whisk until the yolks and whites are fully combined.

step 2

Heat a nonstick skillet over medium heat and add the butter or oil. Swirl the pan to coat the bottom evenly.

step 3

Once the butter is melted or the oil is hot, pour in the beaten eggs. Use a spatula to stir the eggs gently, pushing the cooked edges towards the center of the pan and letting the uncooked eggs flow to the edges.

step 4

When the omelette is mostly set but still slightly runny on top, add any desired fillings such as shredded cheese, diced ham, sliced mushrooms, or chopped herbs to one half of the omelette.

step 5

Use the spatula to fold the other half of the omelette over the fillings, creating a half-moon shape. Cook for another 30 seconds to 1 minute, until the cheese is melted and the omelette is fully cooked.

step 6

Slide the omelette onto a plate and serve hot.

ingredients

2 eggs

Salt and pepper

1 tablespoon butter or oil

Optional: shredded cheese, diced ham, sliced mushrooms, chopped herbs

tools

Mixing bowl

Fork or whisk

Nonstick stillet

Spatula
