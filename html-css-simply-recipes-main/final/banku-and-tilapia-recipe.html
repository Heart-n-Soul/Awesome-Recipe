Banku and Grilled Tilapia

Banku and tilapia is a popular dish in West Africa, particularly in Ghana. Banku is a traditional Ghanaian dish made from fermented corn and cassava dough, while tilapia is a freshwater fish commonly found in West African waters. The combination of banku and tilapia has become a staple dish in Ghanaian cuisine, often served with a spicy pepper sauce and fried plantains. The dish is believed to have originated from the coastal regions of Ghana, where fresh fish is abundant. It is now enjoyed across the country and is considered a national dish of Ghana. The combination of the soft and slightly sour banku with the tender and flavorful tilapia, along with the spicy pepper sauce, makes for a satisfying and delicious meal.

prep time

30 min.

cook time

45 min.

serving

4 servings

Tags : Breakfast Lunch Dinner Dessert

instructions

step 1

In a large pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil.

step 2

In a separate bowl, mix the cornmeal and cassava flour with 1 cup of water until you get a smooth and elastic dough.

step 3

Slowly add the dough to the boiling water, stirring constantly with a wooden spoon until the mixture becomes a thick and smooth paste.

step 4

Reduce the heat and continue to stir until the banku becomes firm and elastic, about 20 minutes. Add salt to taste.

step 5

In a mortar and pestle, grind the onion, tomato, garlic, ginger, and red pepper flakes until you get a smooth paste.

step 6

In a separate bowl, mix the paste with the tomato paste and vegetable oil.

step 7

Rub the tilapia with the spice mixture, making sure to coat the fish thoroughly.

step 8

Preheat the grill or oven to 400°F (200°C).

step 9

Grill or bake the tilapia until golden brown and cooked through, about 15-20 minutes.

step 10

Serve the banku and tilapia hot, with the spicy pepper sauce and fried plantains if desired.

ingredients

2 cups cornmeal

1 cup cassava flour

4 cups water

Salt to taste

4 medium-sized tilapia, cleaned and scaled

2 large onions, chopped

3 large tomatoes, chopped

3 garlic cloves, minced

1 tablespoon ginger, grated

2 tablespoons tomato paste

1 tablespoon red pepper flakes

1 tablespoon vegetable oil

Salt to taste

tools

Large pot

Wooden spoon

Mortar and pestle

Grill or oven
