Burger

Burgers are a popular food item that has been enjoyed for over a century around the world. The origin of burgers can be traced back to the late 1800s when German immigrants brought the concept of ground beef patties to the United States. The modern burger as we know it today, however, is largely credited to the fast-food industry, which popularized the hamburger in the 20th century. Today, burgers are enjoyed in many different ways, from fast-food chains to high-end restaurants, and are a staple of backyard barbecues and casual dining. The classic burger consists of a beef patty, lettuce, tomato, onion, pickles, and condiments such as ketchup, mustard, and mayonnaise, all served between two buns.

prep time

10 min.

cook time

10-12 min.

serving

4 servings

Tags : Breakfast Lunch Dinner Dessert

instructions

step 1

Preheat a grill or a grill pan over medium-high heat. In a large bowl, mix the ground beef, salt, and black pepper until well combined. Divide the mixture into four equal portions and shape them into patties.

step 2

Brush the grill or grill pan with vegetable oil. Add the beef patties and cook for 5-6 minutes on each side, or until they are cooked through to your desired level of doneness

step 3

In the last minute of cooking, add the cheese slices to the top of the patties and let them melt.

step 4

While the patties are cooking, lightly toast the hamburger buns. Spread mayonnaise and ketchup on the bottom bun, then place a lettuce leaf, a tomato slice, and an onion slice on top.

step 5

Place the cooked beef patty with melted cheese on top of the vegetables, then cover with the top bun. Serve immediately.

ingredients

1 pound ground beef (80% lean)

1 teaspoon salt

1/2 teaspoon black pepper

4 hamburger buns

4 slices of cheese

4 lettuce leaves

4 tomato slices

4 onion slices

4 tablespoons mayonnaise

4 tablespoons ketchup

1 tablespoon vegtable oil

tools

Large bowl

Grill or grill pan

Brush

Spatula

Tongs

Knife
