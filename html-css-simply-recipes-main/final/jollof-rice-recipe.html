Jollof Recipe

Jollof rice is a popular and beloved dish in West Africa, especially in Nigeria, Ghana, and Senegal. Its origin is a subject of debate, with different countries claiming to have created the recipe. However, it is widely accepted that the dish was born out of the need to create a flavorful and filling meal that could be shared among a large group of people. The name "Jollof" is believed to have originated from the Wolof people of Senegal, who have a similar dish called "thi√©boudienne." The dish consists of long-grain rice cooked with a blend of tomatoes, onions, and spices, giving it a vibrant reddish-orange color. It is often served with a variety of protein options such as chicken, beef, or fish, and is a staple dish for many West African families and communities.

prep time

30 min.

cook time

45 min.

serving

6-8 servings

Tags : Breakfast Lunch Dinner Dessert

instructions

step 1

Rinse the rice in cold water until the water runs clear, then set it aside to drain.

step 2

In a blender or food processor, blend the onions, tomatoes, red bell peppers, and Scotch bonnet peppers (if using) until smooth.

step 3

In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the blended mixture and cook for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the mixture has thickened and the liquid has reduced.

step 4

Add the minced garlic, ground ginger, ground thyme, curry powder, smoked paprika, salt, and black pepper to the pot, stirring until the spices are well combined with the sauce. Cook for another 2-3 minutes.

step 5

Add the drained rice to the pot and stir to coat the rice with the sauce. Add the water or chicken broth, stirring to combine. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for about 25-30 minutes or until the rice is tender and the liquid has been absorbed.

step 6

Once done, fluff the rice with a fork and serve hot.
