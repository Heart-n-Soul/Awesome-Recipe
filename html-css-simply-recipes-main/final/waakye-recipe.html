Waakye

Waakye is a popular dish in Ghana that has a rich cultural history. The name "waakye" is derived from the words "waa" which means beans and "kye" which means rice in the Ghanaian language of Twi. The dish is believed to have originated in the northern regions of Ghana, where it was a staple food of the Hausa people. Over time, it spread to other regions of Ghana and became a popular dish for breakfast and lunch. Waakye is known for its unique taste, which is derived from the use of red sorghum leaves or millet leaves, which are added to the cooking water for the rice and beans. The leaves give the dish a distinctive brownish color and a slightly nutty flavor.

prep time

30 min.

cook time

60 min.

serving

6-8 servings

Tags : Breakfast Lunch Dinner Dessert

instructions

step 1

Rinse the rice and black-eyed peas separately in cold water and drain.

step 2

In a large pot, boil about 8 cups of water with 1 tablespoon of baking soda and 1 teaspoon of salt. Add the black-eyed peas and let it cook for about 20-25 minutes or until the peas are tender.

step 3

Drain the cooked black-eyed peas and set aside. In the same pot, add the rice, 1 tablespoon of baking soda, and 1-2 teaspoons of salt. Add enough water to cook the rice, cover the pot and let it cook for about 30-35 minutes or until the rice is tender.

step 4

In a separate saucepan, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the chopped onion and saut√© until it becomes translucent. Add the chopped tomatoes, ground ginger, ground garlic, ground pepper, ground aniseed and stir until the tomatoes are cooked and the sauce is thickened.

step 5

Add the cooked black-eyed peas to the tomato sauce and stir until the peas are well coated with the sauce. Add salt to taste.

step 6

To serve, place a scoop of rice and a scoop of the black-eyed peas and tomato sauce on a plate. You can also serve it with boiled eggs, fried plantains, and salad.

ingredients

2 cups of rice

1 cup of black-eyed peas

2 tablespoons of baking soda

2-3 teaspoons of salt

1 large onion, chopped

3 large tomatoes, chopped

2 teaspoon of ground ginger

2 teaspoons of ground garlic

1 teaspoon of ground pepper

1 teaspoon of ground aniseed

2 tablespoons of vegetable oil

tools

Large pot with lid

Saucepan

Colander

Knife and cutting board

Wooden spoon or spatula for stirring
